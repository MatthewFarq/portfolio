While the violin will always have a special place in my heart, it wasn’t until I was pursuing a Masters at Boston Conservatory that I began to reevaluate what I wanted in a professional career. Through my musical background, I learned what it takes to pour myself into my work and to empathise with the human experience through music. I began to develop a craving to challenge my personal biases with solid evidence and facts. This, and a fascination with the growing tech industry, ignited an interest in the incredible world of UX. As a musician who always found value in studying the market, I saw UX design as a means of designing beautifully viable products.

My new perspective pulled me to change the direction that my career was going. Within a year, I left Boston Conservatory, my wife and I purchased a house in Minneapolis and soon adopted a beautiful golden retriever puppy named Charlie Parker. My wife got a job as a software developer at Best Buy, and I got a job at a local music store while I began taking steps towards a career in UX.

This experience encouraged me to take the necessary steps to achieve a career as a UX/UI designer.

While working as the primary sales representative at the music store, it was my responsibility to create online product listings on Amazon and Reverb, and to assist all customers. While answering questions about products with potential customers, I eventually became more and more familiar with common questions that prevented customers from simply purchasing the item online without any assistance. I made it my goal to design flawless product listings with all of the necessary information to enable customers to make the purchase. Shortly after doing this, the music shop experienced a significant increase in online sales. This experience encouraged me to take the necessary steps to achieve a career as a UX/UI designer.

Aside from UX, I enjoy playing with my dog, practicing yoga, and cooking and eating delicious food with my wife. I also enjoy traveling and reading books that challenge how I think about the world.

If you’re interested in working with someone who values the importance of designing user centered products while improving the ROI of the business, please reach out. I’d love to talk with you.

From the cozy tundra of Minneapolis
